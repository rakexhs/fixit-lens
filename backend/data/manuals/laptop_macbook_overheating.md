## Understanding MacBook overheating

MacBooks can run hot under heavy load (video export, compiling code, gaming) and this is often normal, but a laptop that is hot even at idle, or one that shows a "Service Battery" or thermal warning message, may have an underlying issue worth checking with safe external steps first.

## Step 1: Check Activity Monitor for runaway processes

Open Activity Monitor and sort by CPU. A single process pinned near 100% for a long time while idle is a common, safe-to-fix cause of unexpected heat and fan noise. Quitting or restarting that application often resolves it immediately.

## Step 2: Check for blocked vents

MacBook Pro and Air models exhaust heat through vents near the hinge. Make sure the laptop is on a hard, flat, well-ventilated surface, not on a soft surface like a bed or couch, which can block airflow and trap heat.

## Step 3: Check for and install macOS updates

Apple periodically ships thermal management and power efficiency fixes in macOS updates. Check System Settings/Preferences for available updates from Apple's official update mechanism only.

## Step 4: Reset SMC (external, no disassembly)

On many Intel MacBooks, resetting the System Management Controller (SMC) using the official key-combination procedure from Apple's support site can resolve fan and thermal irregularities. This is done entirely externally with no tools or disassembly required.

## High-risk refusal: swollen battery

If the trackpad feels raised, the bottom case appears to be bulging or separating, or the laptop case will not sit flat on a table, this indicates a **swollen lithium-ion battery**. This is a fire and chemical burn hazard. Do not press on the swollen area, do not attempt to remove or puncture the battery yourself, and do not continue using or charging the laptop. Power it off, avoid physical pressure on the case, keep it away from heat, and take it to an authorized Apple service provider or qualified battery-disposal facility immediately. No procedural repair steps are appropriate for this situation outside of a certified technician.
