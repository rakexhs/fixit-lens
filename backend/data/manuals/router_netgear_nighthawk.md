## Understanding the Internet LED colors

On Netgear Nighthawk routers, the globe/Internet LED indicates connection status. A **solid white or green light** means a healthy internet connection. An **amber (orange) light** means the router is connected to the modem but the internet connection itself is degraded or not fully established. A **solid red light** or the light being off entirely means there is no internet connection detected at all.

## Step 1: Verify cable connections

Check that the yellow Ethernet cable from your modem is plugged into the Nighthawk's Internet port (not one of the LAN ports), and that it is fully clicked in at both ends. Loose cabling is the most frequent cause of an amber or red internet light.

## Step 2: Power cycle modem and router

Unplug both the modem and the router from power. Wait at least 30 seconds. Plug the modem back in first and wait until its status lights stabilize (usually 1-2 minutes). Then plug the router back in and allow 2 minutes for it to fully restart. This resolves most amber/red light issues by forcing a fresh handshake with your ISP.

## Step 3: Firmware update caution

Netgear periodically releases firmware updates that can affect connection stability. Firmware updates can be checked from the router's web admin page. Do not interrupt a firmware update once started (do not unplug power) as this can corrupt the router's firmware and require a more involved recovery process. Only start a firmware update if you have a stable power source and can wait for it to finish uninterrupted.

## Step 4: Reset caution

A factory reset (holding the recessed Reset button for 7+ seconds) will erase your custom Wi-Fi name, password, and any port-forwarding rules. Only perform a factory reset after simpler steps have failed, and be ready to reconfigure the router afterward.

## Safety note

These troubleshooting steps only involve power cycling and checking cables; no internal disassembly or electrical risk is involved.
