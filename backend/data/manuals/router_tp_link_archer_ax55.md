## Understanding the Internet LED

The Archer AX55 has a status LED labeled "Internet". A solid **red Internet light** or a **blinking red light** means the router cannot reach the internet through the WAN (modem) connection, even though the router itself is powered on. This is one of the most common issues reported for this model and is almost always resolved without opening the device.

## Step 1: Check the WAN cable

Confirm the Ethernet cable running from your modem into the router's blue "WAN/Internet" port is fully seated at both ends. A loose or damaged Ethernet cable is the single most common cause of a red Internet light. Try re-seating the cable, or swap it for a known-good Ethernet cable if one is available.

## Step 2: Restart modem and router in order

Power off the router first, then power off the modem. Wait 30 seconds. Power the modem back on and wait roughly 60-90 seconds for its own internet/online light to turn solid green or white. Then power the router back on and wait 1-2 minutes for it to fully boot. Restarting in the correct order (modem first, then router) resolves the majority of "no internet" cases because it forces both devices to renegotiate a fresh connection with your ISP.

## Step 3: Check for an ISP outage

If the modem's own internet light is also red or blinking after a full restart, the outage is most likely upstream with your Internet Service Provider (ISP) rather than the router. Check your ISP's status page or contact them directly. No router-side troubleshooting will fix an upstream ISP outage.

## Step 4: Factory reset as a last resort

If the WAN cable is good, the modem shows a healthy connection, and the router still shows a solid red Internet light after two full restarts, a factory reset may be needed. Press and hold the recessed Reset button on the back of the router for about 5-10 seconds until all LEDs flash, then release. This erases all custom settings, so only use this as a last resort after the simpler steps above have failed, and be prepared to redo your Wi-Fi network name and password afterward.

## Safety note

All steps above only involve unplugging power adapters and Ethernet cables. There is no risk of electric shock, fire, or injury from these steps, and no tools are required.
