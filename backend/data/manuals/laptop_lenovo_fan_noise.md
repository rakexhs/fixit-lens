## Understanding fan noise and overheating

Loud, continuous fan noise combined with a hot chassis usually means the laptop's cooling system is working overtime because of dust buildup, blocked vents, or heavy background processes. On most Lenovo laptops this is safe to diagnose and partially fix externally.

## Step 1: Check and clean external vents

With the laptop powered off and unplugged, use a can of compressed air to blow out the exterior vents (usually on the bottom and side/back edges) in short bursts. Hold fans steady from the outside if you can see them spin, to avoid over-spinning the fan bearing. This does not require opening the laptop.

## Step 2: Check for elevated CPU/background load

Open Task Manager (Windows) or Activity Monitor and sort by CPU usage. Heavy background processes, stuck updates, or malware scans can keep fans running at full speed. Closing unnecessary applications often reduces fan noise significantly.

## Step 3: Ensure vents are not physically blocked

Make sure the laptop is used on a hard, flat surface rather than a bed, blanket, or pillow, which can block intake and exhaust vents and cause overheating even with clean internals.

## Step 4: Firmware/BIOS note

Some overheating issues are improved by installing the latest BIOS/firmware update from Lenovo's support site, since fan curve behavior is sometimes adjusted in updates. Only install official updates from Lenovo directly.

## Step 5: Internal fan replacement is moderate risk

If external cleaning and software checks do not resolve the noise, the fan itself may need replacement. This requires opening the bottom cover of the laptop and disconnecting/reconnecting a small fan power connector. This should only be attempted by following your exact model's official service manual, using the correct small screwdriver, and observing anti-static precautions, because incorrect disassembly can damage ribbon cables or void warranty coverage.

## Safety note

Do not attempt to open the laptop while it is powered on or plugged in. If you notice a swollen battery (visible bulging of the trackpad or chassis), do not proceed with any internal work; stop and see the high-risk safety guidance for swollen lithium batteries.
