## What the E24 error means

The **E24 error code** on Bosch dishwashers indicates a drain problem: the dishwasher is unable to pump water out fast enough, usually because of a clog somewhere in the drain path (filter, drain hose, or air gap). It is one of the most common Bosch error codes and is frequently fixable without a technician visit.

## Safety caution before you start

Always turn off the dishwasher and, if you will touch any water inside the tub, switch off power at the breaker or unplug the unit, since there may be standing water at the bottom of the tub in contact with internal electrical components. Do not reach into the tub while the dishwasher is powered on.

## Step 1: Clean the accessible filter

Bosch dishwashers have a removable filter assembly at the bottom of the tub, underneath the lower spray arm. Twist and lift out the filter (this does not require tools) and rinse it under running water to remove food debris, grease, and small particles. A clogged filter is the single most common cause of the E24 error.

## Step 2: Check the drain hose for kinks

If accessible without disassembly, check that the drain hose behind or beneath the dishwasher is not kinked or bent sharply, which can restrict water flow. If the dishwasher is installed under a counter, this may only be checked if the unit can be safely pulled out; do not force it.

## Step 3: Check the air gap or garbage disposal knockout

If your dishwasher drains through an air gap fitting on the sink or through a garbage disposal, a clogged air gap or an un-removed disposal knockout plug can cause E24. Clean the air gap cap and confirm the knockout plug was removed during installation if a disposal is connected.

## Step 4: Stop and call a professional if the problem persists

If the E24 error continues after cleaning the filter, checking the drain hose, and checking the air gap, the issue is likely an internal pump, motor, or wiring fault. Do not disassemble the pump housing or motor yourself. Stop here and contact a qualified appliance technician, since further diagnosis requires opening the appliance's internal water and electrical systems.
