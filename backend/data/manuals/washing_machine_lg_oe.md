## What the OE error means

The **OE error code** (sometimes shown as "OE" flashing on the display) on LG washing machines means the machine detects it cannot drain water out within the expected time. This is almost always caused by a blockage in the drain filter, drain hose, or drain pump area rather than a serious mechanical failure.

## Safety caution before you start

Unplug the washing machine from the electrical outlet before opening the drain filter door, since water may spill out and you do not want water contacting a live outlet or power cord. Keep towels nearby to catch water and never touch the machine or filter with wet hands while it remains plugged in.

## Step 1: Locate and clean the drain filter

Most LG front-load washers have a small drain filter access door at the bottom front of the machine. Open it slowly (water will drain out, so have a shallow pan and towels ready), unscrew the drain filter cap, and remove any lint, coins, buttons, or debris. Rinse the filter and reinsert it, making sure it is screwed back in firmly to avoid leaks.

## Step 2: Check the drain hose

Check that the drain hose at the back of the machine is not kinked, crushed, or blocked, and that it is not positioned higher than the maximum height specified for your model, which can prevent proper drainage.

## Step 3: Run a drain/spin cycle to confirm

After cleaning the filter and checking the hose, run a drain or spin-only cycle to confirm water now drains normally and the OE code does not reappear.

## Step 4: Stop if there is an electrical smell or leak near an outlet

If you notice a burning smell, smoke, or see water pooling near an electrical outlet or power cord at any point, stop immediately, do not touch the machine, unplug it only if you can do so without touching water, and contact a qualified appliance technician or electrician. Do not continue troubleshooting the drain pump or internal components yourself in this situation.
